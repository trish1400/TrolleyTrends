Why has this site been created?

Mainly, to see if I could.

The idea behind it was to enable you to understand your own grocery shopping trends via the loyalty card data that supermarkets collect.

It was important to me that the visualisation be done 'client-side' (javascript), so all the processing is done without your data leaving your browser.

I also hoped that, should it be possible to sufficently anonymise the data client-side, users may be willing to contribute to build a dataset that may enable us to better understand the cost of living crisis. Data has vast benefits and organisations that are sufficiently large enough to capture and analyse vast amounts of data, can have an unfair advantages.

What loyalty card data does it work with?

Presently, only Tesco Clubcard data.

I've requested my data for a couple of other supermarket loyalty cards which I have, but I've not yet received the data and I'm not sure if, When I do receive it whether it will be in a standardised format or not.

With thanks to...

GDPR: I'll fess up to being a massive GDPR fan. Your right to the data held about you in a portable, machine-readable format is enshrined under GDPR, without that, this would not be possible. The clarity it provided on what is considered Personally Identifiable Information (PPI), what it means for data to be psuedo-anonymous vs anonymous also makes it possible to think about GPDR as an enabling force, rather than, as it often is, a limiting one.

Tesco: For taking the enlightened approach of making the process of requesting and receiving your data automatic. Without that I would not have bothered.

OpenAI - ChatGPT: I'm not a programmer, ChatGPT created most of this (even the logo), while also teaching me and assisting with my frustrations. The future is here and it is wild.

The makers: The various indivduals and organisations that invest time and energy into creating frameworks that they then allow anyone to use under very permissable licences. It is to stand on the shoulders of giants, namely Bootstrap, CoreUI, Chart.js, Animate on Scroll and heroicons
